About us

We care about the little things.

LaceMate was founded by a team of engineering students at Imperial College London with the goal of making a little bit easier for those we can help. Our goal is to create a device that can tie laces with minimal need for fine motor control.
Over 30 million people suffer from multiple sclerosis (MS), cerebral palsy (CP), muscular dystrophy (MD) or Parkinson's disease (PD) worldwide. These diseases, along with many other conditions, often result in reduced fine motor control. Whilst solutions exist to help with many of the big problems that such conditions can bring, there are few products out there to assist with minor every-day tasks, like tying your shoelaces.
Solutions like Velcro or slip-on shoes mean that you can't just wear whatever shoes you want. This may sound insignificant but for children and teenagers who just want to fit in with their friends, it can be a big deal! And because we care about the little things, it's a big deal to us as well!
